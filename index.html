Step three

Email the right person.

Councils don't run health or benefit services (that's the HSE and the Department of Social Protection) — but councillors deal with this every week and know the local doors. For entitlements, citizensinformation.ie is the best first stop.

The guide's rule: email your best match first, and copy one councillor from a different party — two doors beat one.

Step four

What happens after you send

  1. This week. A reply usually comes within days — answering residents is the job. If they act, say thanks: thanked constituents get helped again.
  2. Two weeks, no reply? Send the follow-up below. Most people never follow up — which is exactly why following up works.
  3. A month, still stuck? Email a second councillor from a different party (pick one from your list above) with the same letter, adding one line: "I first raised this with Councillor [name] on [date]."
  4. Check the record, not the promises. Whatever happens next is public — the council's meeting pages show what was actually raised.
